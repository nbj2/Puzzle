The core insight

Every career tool starts with the same question: what do you want? Puzzle starts with what you'll never tolerate again. That signal is more durable than aspiration — and it's always true, even when someone doesn't know what they want next.

The second insight: the conversation adapts in real time. Every question is written by AI after reading everything the person has said so far. Someone who says "I'm just curious" gets a completely different conversation than someone who says "I know exactly what I want." Same data collected. Completely different experience. No other career tool does this.

The third insight: the vocabulary gap is the product. Most people can't describe their skills in language the job market understands. Puzzle closes that gap — not by teaching them, but by reflecting their own words back in a frame the outside world recognizes. The "How to say it in an interview" bridge sentence on every job card is the most valuable single feature in the product.

Four people the product was built for

These are simulated personas used to stress-test the product across different backgrounds, industries, and stuck-ness profiles. Each one represents a real demographic that existing tools systematically fail.

Persona 01 — Healthcare
Marcus, 38
Dialysis technician · 9 years · Evansville, Indiana
"You've been doing the 70% of the job nobody trains for and nobody measures — and there's a job title for that. You caught something no monitor did. That's not clinical skill. That's patient advocacy, and there are companies that built a business around hiring exactly that."
What previous tools gave him: REI, Patagonia, Airbnb.
What Puzzle gave him: Healthcare navigator roles within 25 miles of his home, with bridge sentences translating "I hook patients up to dialysis machines" into language health system HR actually searches for.
Why it matters: 18 million healthcare support workers in the US. Almost none of them have job market vocabulary for what they actually do.
Persona 02 — Public Sector
Renata, 41
Juvenile probation officer · 6 years · Pueblo, Colorado
"The Devon memo is the whole story — you wrote something nobody asked for, in language the system understands, because you saw what the paperwork was missing. You've been told caring too much is a liability. Precisely calibrated is not the same thing as too emotional."
What she needed: Someone to name the translation gap. She knew what she valued and where she wanted to go — she just didn't have the language.
What Puzzle gave her: Victim advocacy and program coordinator roles at organizations in Pueblo, plus interview language drawn directly from her own description of her work.
Why it matters: 7 million public sector workers in the US are leaving their fields. Most don't know how to describe their skills outside of government job titles.
Persona 03 — Hospitality / Operations
Terrell, 33
Restaurant GM · 4 years · Knoxville, Tennessee
"What you did in November — that isn't restaurant management. That's what people with MBAs pay to learn how to do and still often can't. You need an environment that has a name for it. Your background isn't something to translate. It's the credential."

What Puzzle gave him: Toast — a company literally built for operators with his background — as his top match. First time his experience was the credential, not the obstacle.
Why it matters: 15 million hospitality workers. The industry's only career narrative is "own a place someday." Puzzle is the first tool that offers a different story.

What's honest — the gaps
What a good investor will ask, and the real answers:
⚠️Real users: Currently zero paying users. The four personas above are simulated stress tests, not real testimonials. The product works — it needs real people to prove it converts.
⚠️Paid tier: The $12/month infrastructure doesn't exist yet. Stripe, Supabase, and email delivery are the next build. The conversation and matches are fully working.
⚠️Job data: Matches are AI-generated with LinkedIn/Indeed search links. No employer partnerships or proprietary job data yet. That's a real moat to build.
⚠️Team: First-time founder, no technical co-founder. Built with AI assistance. That's the origin story — and it's also the risk.
✓What is real: The core product works. The insight is differentiated. The build cost is extraordinary. The market is enormous. And the four personas above are real proof that the product does something no existing tool does.

What we're looking for

The right first investor for Puzzle isn't writing a $10M check. They're someone who has felt this problem — either as a person who was good at something a job description couldn't capture, or as someone who has tried to hire for that kind of person and failed.

The immediate need is $25K–$50K to cover API costs at scale, build the paid tier infrastructure, and get to 500 real users with measurable conversion data. That number buys six months and a real answer on whether people pay.

What we're not looking for: someone who wants to scale before the product is proven. The conversation is the product. It has to stay honest, specific, and human — or it becomes everything it was built against.
